Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm)

The Bachelor of Pharmacy is a 4-year undergraduate program that provides comprehensive education in pharmaceutical sciences, preparing students for diverse careers in the pharmacy profession.

Course Overview

The B.Pharm program is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in pharmaceutical sciences including Pharmaceutics, pharmaceutical chemistry, pharmacognosy, and pharmaceutical analysis along with specialized pharmaceutical subjects. The curriculum combines theoretical knowledge with practical training to develop competent pharmacy professionals.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Candidates must have passed 10+2 examination with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Mathematics
  • Minimum aggregate of 50% marks (45% for SC/ST candidates)
  • Age limit: Minimum 17 years as on 31st December of the admission year
  • For Govt Seat Must qualify in relevant entrance examinations (NEET/State CET)

Duration & Structure

  • Duration: 4 years (8 semesters)
  • Intake: 60 seats
  • Medium of Instruction: English
  • Attendance: Minimum 80% attendance required

Curriculum

Year 1

  • Human Anatomy & Physiology
  • Pharmaceutical Analysis
  • Pharmaceutics
  • Pharmaceutical Inorganic Chemistry
  • Communication Skills
  • Remedial Biology/Mathematics

Year 2

  • Pharmaceutical Organic Chemistry
  • Physical Pharmaceutics
  • Pharmaceutical Microbiology
  • Pharmaceutical Engineering
  • Computer Applications in Pharmacy
  • Environmental Sciences

Year 3

  • Pharmaceutical Organic Chemistry
  • Medicinial Chemistry
  • Physical Pharmaceutics
  • Pharmaceutical Microbiology
  • Pharmaceutical Biotechnology
  • Pharmacognosy & Phytochemistry

Year 4

  • Pharmaceutical Jurisprudence
  • Pharmaceutical Marketing
  • Pharmaceutical Biotechnology
  • Biostatistics & Research Methodology
  • Pharmacology & Toxicology
  • Novel Drug Delivery System

Career Opportunities

B.Pharm graduates can pursue careers in:

  • Pharmaceutical Industry (Production, Quality Control, R&D)
  • Hospital Pharmacy
  • Community Pharmacy
  • Drug Regulatory Affairs
  • Clinical Research
  • Pharmaceutical Marketing
  • Academia and Research
  • Government Services (Drug Inspector, etc.)

Further Studies

B.Pharm graduates can pursue higher education including M.Pharm, MBA in Pharmaceutical Management, or Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Sciences to enhance their career prospects and specialization.

Quick Facts

Seats: 60

Eligibility: 10+2 with PCM/PCB

Medium: English

Affiliation: RGUHS, Karnataka
