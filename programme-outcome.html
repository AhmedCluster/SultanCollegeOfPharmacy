Programme Outcomes

At SJM College of Pharmacy, our educational programs are designed to develop well-rounded pharmacy professionals equipped with the knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary for success in the pharmaceutical industry and healthcare sector. Below are the expected outcomes for our various programs:

Diploma in Pharmacy (D.Pharm) Programme Outcomes

Upon completion of the D.Pharm program, graduates will be able to:

  1. Demonstrate knowledge of pharmaceutical sciences and their applications in various pharmacy settings
  2. Prepare and dispense medications following established procedures
  3. Provide appropriate information and counseling to patients about medication use
  4. Maintain accurate records of drugs and assist in inventory management
  5. Comply with legal and ethical requirements in pharmacy practice
  6. Communicate effectively with patients and healthcare professionals
  7. Apply basic knowledge of drug formulation and quality control
  8. Demonstrate awareness of community health issues and preventive healthcare

Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm) Programme Outcomes

Upon completion of the B.Pharm program, graduates will be able to:

  1. Apply knowledge of pharmaceutical sciences in practice settings
  2. Design, formulate and evaluate pharmaceutical products
  3. Identify, analyze, and solve complex pharmaceutical problems
  4. Conduct pharmaceutical research and interpret research findings
  5. Use modern techniques, skills, and tools necessary for pharmacy practice
  6. Practice pharmacy with professional ethics and integrity
  7. Communicate effectively with patients, healthcare professionals, and the community
  8. Engage in lifelong learning and adapt to evolving pharmaceutical technologies
  9. Understand the impact of pharmaceutical solutions in societal and environmental contexts
  10. Function effectively as an individual and as a member of multidisciplinary teams
  11. Demonstrate knowledge of contemporary issues in pharmacy

Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D) Programme Outcomes

Upon completion of the Pharm.D program, graduates will be able to:

  1. Provide patient-centered care through medication therapy management and disease state management
  2. Design evidence-based therapeutic regimens and monitoring plans
  3. Identify and resolve medication-related problems
  4. Collaborate with interprofessional healthcare teams to optimize patient outcomes
  5. Critically evaluate medical literature and apply evidence-based medicine principles
  6. Develop and implement population-based pharmaceutical care programs
  7. Demonstrate leadership skills in various pharmacy practice settings
  8. Conduct clinical research and contribute to the advancement of pharmacy practice
  9. Educate patients, caregivers, and other healthcare professionals about medications
  10. Practice pharmacy with the highest ethical standards and professional integrity
  11. Engage in continuous professional development and lifelong learning

Master of Pharmacy (M.Pharm) Programme Outcomes

Upon completion of the M.Pharm program, graduates will be able to:

  1. Demonstrate advanced knowledge in specialized areas of pharmaceutical sciences
  2. Design and conduct independent research in pharmaceutical sciences
  3. Analyze complex pharmaceutical problems and develop innovative solutions
  4. Apply advanced techniques and modern tools for pharmaceutical research and development
  5. Critically evaluate scientific literature and research methodologies
  6. Communicate research findings effectively through publications and presentations
  7. Develop leadership skills for managing pharmaceutical research and development
  8. Demonstrate awareness of intellectual property rights and regulatory affairs
  9. Engage in lifelong learning and adapt to emerging technologies in pharmaceutical sciences
  10. Apply ethical principles in research and professional practice

Specialization-Specific Outcomes

M.Pharm in Pharmaceutics
  • Design and develop novel drug delivery systems
  • Optimize pharmaceutical formulations using advanced techniques
  • Apply principles of industrial pharmacy and scale-up techniques
  • Conduct bioavailability and bioequivalence studies
M.Pharm in Pharmaceutical Chemistry
  • Design and synthesize novel drug molecules
  • Perform structural elucidation of drug molecules using spectroscopic techniques
  • Develop and validate analytical methods for drug substances and products
  • Apply computational approaches in drug design and development
M.Pharm in Pharmacology
  • Design and conduct preclinical studies for drug development
  • Evaluate pharmacological and toxicological profiles of drug molecules
  • Apply molecular and cellular techniques in pharmacological research
  • Interpret clinical trial data and pharmacovigilance reports

Ph.D in Pharmaceutical Sciences Programme Outcomes

Upon completion of the Ph.D program, graduates will be able to:

  1. Demonstrate comprehensive understanding of pharmaceutical sciences and related disciplines
  2. Identify significant research problems and formulate research hypotheses
  3. Design and execute original research that contributes to the advancement of pharmaceutical sciences
  4. Apply advanced research methodologies and analytical techniques
  5. Critically analyze and interpret complex research data
  6. Communicate research findings through high-quality publications in peer-reviewed journals
  7. Present and defend research outcomes in scientific forums
  8. Demonstrate expertise in a specialized area of pharmaceutical sciences
  9. Develop independent thinking and problem-solving abilities
  10. Mentor and guide junior researchers and students
  11. Apply ethical principles in research and scholarly activities
  12. Secure research funding and manage research projects

Graduate Attributes

In addition to program-specific outcomes, all graduates from SJM College of Pharmacy are expected to demonstrate the following attributes:

Professional Knowledge

Graduates will possess comprehensive knowledge in pharmaceutical sciences and apply this knowledge to solve complex problems in healthcare settings.

Critical Thinking

Graduates will demonstrate the ability to critically analyze information, evaluate evidence, and make informed decisions in pharmaceutical practice and research.

Ethical Conduct

Graduates will adhere to the highest standards of professional ethics, integrity, and responsibility in all aspects of pharmacy practice and research.

Communication Skills

Graduates will effectively communicate pharmaceutical information to patients, healthcare professionals, and the community using appropriate verbal, written, and digital means.

Lifelong Learning

Graduates will engage in continuous professional development and adapt to evolving pharmaceutical knowledge, technologies, and practices throughout their careers.

Leadership and Teamwork

Graduates will demonstrate leadership qualities and the ability to work effectively in multidisciplinary teams to achieve common goals in healthcare settings.
