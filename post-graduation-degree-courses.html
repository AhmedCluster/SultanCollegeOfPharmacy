Post Graduation (PG) Degree Courses

Master of Pharmacy (M.Pharm)

The Master of Pharmacy (M.Pharm) is a 2-year postgraduate degree program that offers specialized knowledge in various branches of pharmaceutical sciences. Our college offers M.Pharm in the following specializations:

M.Pharm in Pharmaceutics

Duration: 2 years (4 semesters)

Eligibility: B.Pharm degree with minimum 55% marks

Intake: 15 seats

Description: This specialization focuses on the science and technology of formulating, manufacturing, and evaluating pharmaceutical dosage forms. Students gain expertise in drug delivery systems, pharmaceutical technology, and industrial pharmacy.

M.Pharm in Pharmaceutical Chemistry

Duration: 2 years (4 semesters)

Eligibility: B.Pharm degree with minimum 55% marks

Intake: 15 seats

Description: This specialization focuses on the chemistry of pharmaceutical compounds, drug design, synthesis, and analysis. Students learn advanced techniques in medicinal chemistry and drug development.

M.Pharm in Pharmacology

Duration: 2 years (4 semesters)

Eligibility: B.Pharm degree with minimum 55% marks

Intake: 15 seats

Description: This specialization focuses on the study of drug action, drug safety, and efficacy. Students gain expertise in preclinical and clinical research methodologies, toxicology, and pharmacodynamics.

Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D)

We also offer Ph.D programs in various disciplines of pharmaceutical sciences. For more information about our Ph.D program, please visit the Ph.D page.

Admission Process

Admission to M.Pharm programs is based on:

  • GPAT (Graduate Pharmacy Aptitude Test) score
  • Academic performance in B.Pharm
  • Personal interview

For detailed information about the admission process, eligibility criteria, and fee structure, please visit our Admissions page.
